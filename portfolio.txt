    <section id="goalfi">
        <div class="container">
            <h2>Case Study: GoalFi Games</h2>
            <div class="project-card">
                <h3>Building a Web3 Gaming Platform from Scratch</h3>
                <p class="project-meta">March 2024 - August 2024 | Founder & Product Lead</p>
                
                <h4 style="margin-top: 30px; margin-bottom: 15px;">The Problem</h4>
                <p>
                    Gamers constantly debate who's better at football games, but there was no way to back up claims 
                    with real stakes. I wanted to create a platform where gamers could "put their money where their 
                    mouth is"—betting on their own skills, not just luck.
                </p>
                
                <h4 style="margin-top: 30px; margin-bottom: 15px;">The Solution</h4>
                <p>
                    GoalFi Games: A Web3-integrated football gaming platform where players use $GOLS tokens to 
                    bet on skill-based matches, buy players, and earn rewards. Built on BSC blockchain for 
                    transparent, verifiable transactions.
                </p>
                
                <h4 style="margin-top: 30px; margin-bottom: 15px;">My Approach (Learning PM by Doing)</h4>
                <ul style="margin: 20px 0; padding-left: 20px;">
                    <li style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Started with MVP:</strong> Launched the token first to validate demand before building the full gaming platform</li>
                    <li style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Growth Hacking:</strong> Designed viral mechanics through airdrop campaigns and referral incentives</li>
                    <li style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Community-First:</strong> Built engaged community on Telegram and X (Twitter) before product launch</li>
                    <li style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Data-Driven Iteration:</strong> Monitored user feedback and engagement metrics to guide feature decisions</li>
                    <li style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Lean Execution:</strong> Tested assumptions quickly with zero budget, pivoted based on what worked</li>
                </ul>
                
                <div class="evidence-section">
                    <h4>Evidence & Proof Points</h4>
                    <ul class="evidence-list">
                        <li>44,000+ user signups via Google Forms (data file available)</li>
                        <li>Active X (Twitter) account with campaign history</li>
                        <li>Smart contract deployed on BSC blockchain (verifiable on-chain)</li>
                        <li>Telegram community grew to 1,000+ active members</li>
                        <li>Peak token market cap of $53,000</li>
                        <li>Original website (can be reactivated by renewing hosting)</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                
                <div class="learning-section">
                    <h4>What I Learned (The Hard Way)</h4>
                    <p>
                        <strong>Timing is everything:</strong> I underestimated capital needs at the scaling phase. 
                        Growing to 44k users organically was exciting, but I needed funding to build the actual gaming 
                        platform to retain them. The project died not because of lack of interest, but because I 
                        couldn't capitalize on the momentum.
                    </p>
                    <p style="margin-top: 10px;">
                        <strong>Key takeaway:</strong> Product-market fit isn't enough—you need a clear path to 
                        monetization and the resources to execute before growth plateaus. This lesson now drives 
                        how I approach PluggedIn's roadmap and funding strategy.
                    </p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </div>
